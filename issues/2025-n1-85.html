STUDY OF SPATIAL DISTRIBUTION OF ELECTROMAGNETIC FIELDS OF INDUSTRIAL FREQUENCY IN THE CENTRAL PART OF ULYANOVSK

Sturman Vladimir Itskhakovitch, Loginovskaya Alyona Nikolaevna

St. Petersburg State University of Telecommunications named after Prof. M.A. Bonch-Bruevich

Abstract: The study of the spatial distribution of indicators of electromagnetic fields of industrial frequency in the central part of Ulyanovsk showed that the intensity of electric fields is insignificant everywhere, with the exception of the protected zones of high-voltage lines. Increased values of magnetic induction, including those exceeding the approximate safe level of 200-400 nT, are moderately common, mainly in places where public catering enterprises and medical centers are concentrated, which is reflected in the compiled map. The significant spread of recreational areas and the weak development of market trade can be considered as factors that reduce the level of electromagnetic pollution.

Key words: electromagnetic fields; electric fields; intensity; magnetic fields; magnetic induction; Mapping; Ulyanovsk

For citation: Sturman V.I., Loginovskaya A.N. Study of spatial distribution of electromagnetic fields of industrial frequency in the central part of Ulyanovsk. // Astrakhan Bulletin of Ecological Education. 2025, №1 (85): 16-24 DOI 10.36698/2304-5957-2025-1-16-24

Perov Alexander Yurevich, Tillaeva Yulia Igorevna, Taskaev Alexander Olegovich

Kuban State Agrarian University named after I. T. Trubilin

Abstract: Currently, food security of the Russian Federation is one of the main priorities of the state. At the same time, one of the factors in stabilizing the productivity of agricultural land in forest-steppe and steppe regions is shelterbelt afforestation, which was carried out mainly in the period from 1950 to 1980. By now, a significant part of the forest belts has largely lost their protective functions due to such problems as desolation, overgrowth, loss of openwork or blown structures. As a result, the problem of inventorying forest belts and subsequent restoration of their protective functions arose. This paper presents a methodology for studying the protective effect of forest shelterbelts and degradation processes using geoinformation technologies and satellite images, and examines the state of forest shelterbelts in the Korenovsky District of the Krasnodar Territory.
